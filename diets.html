Diet

Keto
The Keto Diet is a low carb high fat diet that involves drastically reducing your
carbohydrate intake and replacing it with fat. The reduction in carbs forces the body
into a metabolic state of ketosis. While in the state of ketosis the body becomes efficient
at burning fat for energy. It also transforms fat into ketones in the liver,which can supply
energy to the brain. Watch the video below for some tasty Keto recipes!
Paleo
The Paleo Diet is a diet that mimics the diet of our ancestors who lived in the
paleolithic period dating from 2.5 million to 10,000 years ago. A paleo diet
includes foods that could be obtained by hunting and gathering which was a prominent
way of life back then. The typical foods consumed on the Paleo diet are lean meats,
fish, fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds. Watch the video below for some tasty Paleo recipes!
Whole30
The Whole30 diet is a 30 day diet that eliminates certain foods and activities from our
daily lifestyles. The rules of the diet are: no sugar real or artificial, no alcohol in any form, no dairy, no grains, no legumes, no processed foods, and no stepping on the scale except
for day 1 and day 30 of the plan. Some exceptions to the rules above that are actually allowed
on the diet are: ghee, clarified butter, fruit juice, green beans, sugar snap pees, snow peas,
coconut aminos, and salt. The Whole30 diet was designed to be simple with no stress over
weighing, measuring, or counting calories.The only thing to be concerned about is making good
food choices which are whole food choices. Watch the video below for some tasty Whole30 recipes!
Vegetarian
The Vegetarian Diet is a diet that eliminates meat, poultry, and fish while allowing the
consumption of dairy products, such as milk, cheese, butter, and eggs. The typical vegetarian diet
consists of fruits, vegetables, dairy, legumes, nuts,seeds, noodles, and tofu.
Watch the video below for some tasty Vegetarian recipes!
Vegan
The Vegan Diet is a diet that eliminates the consumption of all animal products which include: red meat,
poultry, fish, seafood, dairy from all animals, eggs from all animals, all the way down to bee produects,
like honey. Many people become vegan for ethical, religious, environmental, or health reasons. Veganism may seem impossible
at first glance, but with a little research you'll find a suitable replacement vegan option to just about everything.
Watch the video below for some tasty Vegan recipes!
Raw Vegan
The Raw Vegan diet is exacly what its name implies, "Raw Vegan". The elimination of all animal products and
purpose behind becoming a vegan is the same as every other vegan diet. The only caveat, is the consumption of
non cooked food. A typical raw vegan diet consists of: 1. Fruits that are fresh, dried, juiced, or dehydrated.
2. Vegetables that are raw, juiced, or dehydrated. 3. Raw nut milk from almonds or cashews. 4. fermented foods
like miso, sauerkraut, and kimchi. 5. Legumes and grains that have been sprouted or soaked
